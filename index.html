The Work

Mythology, geometry, and the warmth of hand-built form

✦

Mythology

Fanie Simon draws from the rich cosmology of Aztec, Maya, and Hindu traditions. Deities like Chantico, Cihuacoatl, and Ganesha become vessels for exploring universal themes of nature, power, and transformation.

◈

Sacred Geometry

Each composition is built on precise geometric structures — circles, radial forms, and modular grids — that reference sacred architecture and ritual objects while remaining unmistakably contemporary.

◯

Hand-Built Surfaces

Every piece is built by hand in the Basel studio. Wood is cut, shaped, assembled, and finished with mirror, silk, dried flowers, or epoxy — layers that reward close looking and transform with the light.

◆

Basel × Tulum

The work holds two worlds: the precision of Swiss craft and the warmth of Mexican ceremony. This tension between order and ritual is the living edge of Fanie Simon's creative practice.
